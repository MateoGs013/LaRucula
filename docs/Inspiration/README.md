# LaRucula — Inspiration Synthesis

## Reviewed material
This folder contains:
- 4 still references
- 4 screen recordings of hospitality / editorial experiences

The references were reviewed to extract composition, typography, color, and pacing signals rather than to copy any single layout.

For current external benchmark context, pair this document with `docs/awwwards-analysis.md`.

## What the references consistently do
- Use large serif statements as visual anchors, not just headings.
- Pair those statements with very small utility text, creating a strong scale contrast.
- Work with soft off-white canvases, sage or muted green slabs, and deep charcoal sections.
- Let images overlap blocks, escape the grid, or sit inside framed editorial windows.
- Treat interiors, atmosphere, and spatial mood as equal to product or menu content.
- Use monograms, seals, handwritten signatures, or badge-like marks as identity cues.
- Favor long vertical rhythm with intentional pauses over dense content stacking.
- Make the site feel more like a luxury hospitality editorial than a standard restaurant homepage.

## What the references avoid
- Repetitive section recipes.
- Symmetrical “headline + paragraph + image” layouts over and over.
- Loud gradients, neon accents, or trend-driven digital effects.
- Heavy ornament without compositional purpose.
- Card grids that flatten hierarchy.

## Translation for LaRucula
LaRucula should not look like a generic Mediterranean restaurant site.

It should feel like:
- a dining house with authorship
- an editorial hospitality brand
- a place shaped by sea light, architecture, and ritual
- a premium experience with calm tension

That means:
- hero sections should feel composed, layered, and spatial
- food imagery should be present, but not dominate every section
- interiors, materials, and atmosphere should carry real narrative weight
- sage, ivory, toast, and dusk should create a richer tonal range than beige alone
- each section should have a different visual thesis

## Design moves to keep
- floating content cards over photography
- oversized serif headlines with tight measure
- cropped imagery mixed with smaller supporting frames
- dark sections used sparingly for contrast and sophistication
- monogram / seal accents
- handwritten notes in small doses only
- masked reveals and slow parallax instead of basic fade-up everywhere

## Design moves to avoid
- endless food tiles
- hero copy that sits in a generic centered column
- decorative waves or SVG accents with no structural role
- filler gallery strips without narrative purpose
- mobile layouts that just collapse everything into one repeated stack
